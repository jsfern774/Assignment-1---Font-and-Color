Sophisticated

Sophistication originally meant "to simplify". Now is is used to describe refinement, such as having good taste and subtlety in place of poor taste and vulgarity.


Color combinations which are subtle and timeless help to portray sophistication. Black and white is a timeless combination, used in conjunction with subtle marroons and browns help to conjure up imagery of wineries. Cursive typefaces are generally more elegant and sophisticated, while a nice sans-serif font for the body helps promote an enjoyable and simple reading experience.
